# 01 - Site Positioning

## What this site is

A buyer-first home recovery site focused on choosing, installing, and using home saunas. The first site identity should be sauna-specific, with adjacent recovery categories introduced only when they support the sauna buyer journey.

The core reader is choosing between a $300 sauna blanket, a $500-$1,500 portable sauna, a $2,000-$8,000 infrared cabin, or a $3,000-$15,000 outdoor/barrel sauna.

## Recommended positioning

Be the practical home sauna decision guide:

- Which sauna type fits your home, budget, climate, space, and recovery goals.
- Which products are worth considering, and who should avoid them.
- What setup costs, electrical needs, heat-up times, maintenance, and safety constraints buyers overlook.
- How sauna compares with adjacent recovery tools like red light therapy and cold plunge without turning into a generic wellness site.

## What this site is not

- Not a local sauna/spa directory at launch.
- Not a medical advice site.
- Not a generic wellness blog.
- Not a thin affiliate review farm.
- Not a cold plunge or red light site first. Those are expansion lanes.

## Trust angle

The content should win by being more specific than affiliate competitors:

- Real dimensions, clearances, electrical requirements, installation notes, heat-up times, and maintenance costs.
- Product pros and cons by home type: apartment, garage, basement, backyard, small house, rental.
- Clear disclosure when the site has not tested a product.
- No fake ratings, fake testing, or exaggerated health claims.

## Expansion logic

Start narrow with home sauna. Add red light therapy and contrast therapy as adjacent recovery content only after the core sauna pages are live and internally linked.
