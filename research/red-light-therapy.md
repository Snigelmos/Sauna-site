# Red light therapy (photobiomodulation) - evidence + device parameters

For the `red-light-therapy-at-home` adjacency guide. Keep it a practical device-buyer guide. Conservative on claims; evidence is real but heterogeneous, and consumer devices are weaker than clinical systems.

## What it is
Red light therapy (RLT) = photobiomodulation (PBM) = low-level light therapy (LLLT). Uses non-thermal red and near-infrared (NIR) light, ~600-1100 nm "optical window," absorbed mainly by mitochondrial cytochrome-c oxidase -> increased ATP, modulation of reactive oxygen species and inflammation. Not UV; does not damage DNA.
- Umbrella review (Systematic Reviews, Springer 2025): 15 meta-analyses, >9,000 patients, 35 endpoints; roles in pain reduction, wound healing, tissue regeneration, inflammation. Critically: "no existing standard protocol" - wavelengths, dose, sessions vary widely; most underlying meta-analyses rated low quality.
  - https://link.springer.com/article/10.1186/s13643-025-02902-3

## Wavelengths (the two that matter)
- **~660 nm (visible red):** penetrates ~2-5 mm -> skin, collagen/elastin (fibroblasts), hair, surface vessels, wound healing.
- **~850 nm (near-infrared, invisible):** deeper penetration -> muscle, joints, deep tissue.
- Dual 660+850 nm targets multiple depths; clinical trials often use both.
- Source: Comfytemp science explainer; redlight-wellness dosage guide.

## Dose / irradiance (cite as ranges, not prescriptions)
- Irradiance (power density): therapeutic window generally ~10-200 mW/cm2 (too low = nothing; too high = possible tissue stress).
- Dose (energy density, J/cm2): skin ~3-15 J/cm2; muscle recovery ~20-40 J/cm2 (810-850 nm); joint pain ~15-60 J/cm2. Chronic-pain RCTs span ~4 to >100 J/cm2.
- Typical session: ~10-20 min, 2-5x/week; cumulative/repeated protocols associated with more durable effects.
- Distance examples: skin 6-12 in; muscle/joint 2-6 in.

## Evidence by use (conservative)
- **Skin / photoaging (dermatology review, Forum Dermatologicum):** consistent improvement in texture, elasticity, fine-wrinkle reduction with red/NIR; moderate efficacy of blue+red for inflammatory acne; RCTs show increased hair density in androgenetic alopecia (LLLT). Melasma: visible light can worsen hyperpigmentation in darker skin types -> caution/photoprotection. Wound healing/rosacea: preliminary.
  - https://journals.viamedica.pl/forum_dermatologicum/article/view/111321
- **Chronic pain (Frontiers 2026 systematic review):** red/NIR 660-905 nm; many RCTs show analgesic effect (e.g., fibromyalgia whole-body PBM 660/850 nm, 25.2 J/cm2, 20 min, 3x/wk x4 wks showed significant pain reduction), but protocol heterogeneity limits firm conclusions.
  - https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/integrative-neuroscience/articles/10.3389/fnint.2026.1717372/full
- **Consumer vs professional:** consumer devices generally deliver LOWER irradiance/fluence with limited independent validation. Professional systems have more robust evidence. (Forum Dermatologicum review.)

## Safety
Favorable overall; adverse effects mostly mild/transient. Eye protection sensible with high-power panels. Melasma/darker skin: caution with visible light. Not a substitute for medical care. Avoid medical-outcome promises.

## Buyer guidance to build the page around
- Device types: full panels (best power/area), face masks (skin, lower power), handheld/wands (spot), wraps/pads (joints/muscle).
- What to verify: stated wavelength accuracy (true 660/850 nm, not vague "red"), irradiance at a stated distance (mW/cm2), treated area, third-party testing, return policy.
- Realistic expectations: cumulative, modest, condition-dependent; not instant or curative.
- Programs/brands (from monetization doc): Joovv, Mito Red Light, PlatinumLED, HigherDose.
